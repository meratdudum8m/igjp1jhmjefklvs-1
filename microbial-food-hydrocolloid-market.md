<p><p>The microbial food hydrocolloid market encompasses various types that serve as thickening, gelling, and stabilizing agents in food products. Carboxymethylcellulose is a widely used thickener, while gelatin acts as a gelling agent derived from animal sources. Guar gum, from guar beans, enhances texture, and Gum Acacia, or Gum Arabic, is a natural emulsifier. Xanthan gum, produced through fermentation, provides viscosity and stability. Other hydrocolloids may include locust bean gum and pectin, catering to diverse food formulation needs.</p></p>
